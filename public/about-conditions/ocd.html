About OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) involves unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) that a person feels driven to perform. It's not just being neat or organized - OCD causes significant distress and interferes with daily life. The obsessions create anxiety, and the compulsions are attempts to reduce that anxiety or prevent something bad from happening. OCD affects about 1 in 40 adults and often starts in childhood or young adulthood.

Common Symptoms

Obsessions are unwanted thoughts, images, or urges that keep coming back. Common ones include fears about contamination, worries about harm coming to yourself or others, need for things to be "just right," and unwanted sexual or violent thoughts. Compulsions are repetitive behaviors or mental acts like excessive hand washing, checking locks repeatedly, counting, arranging things in a specific order, or repeating words silently. People with OCD know these thoughts and behaviors don't make sense, but they feel unable to stop them.

How It's Treated

OCD responds very well to specific treatments. Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) is a type of therapy that gradually exposes people to their fears while helping them resist doing compulsions. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) helps change the thinking patterns that fuel OCD. Medications called SSRIs (like Prozac or Zoloft) can significantly reduce OCD symptoms. Mindfulness techniques help people observe their thoughts without acting on them. Treatment often combines therapy and medication for best results.

What to Do Next

If OCD symptoms are taking up significant time in your day or causing distress, professional help can make a huge difference. A mental health professional experienced with OCD can provide proper assessment and evidence-based treatment. Remember that OCD is very treatable - most people see significant improvement with the right care. You don't have to live with intrusive thoughts and time-consuming rituals. Effective treatment can help you regain control of your life.
